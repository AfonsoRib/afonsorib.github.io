Technique is going to enslave us all! - A brief view of Jacques Ellul's The Technological Society

March 26, 2022

Watch it in a video format here.

Introduction

Technique is going to enslave us all! We, in the modern world, like to think that we are in control of ourselves. We tell ourselves that we are using Technique, most of the time misunderstood as technology, for our own good. That it is all in good will and for the betterment of mankind. When, In reality, It’s pretty much opposite. We are slaves to it.

What is Technique?

What then is “Technique”? Technique, in ellul’s words, is “the totality of methods rationally arrived at and having absolute efficiency (for a given stage of development) in every field of human activity”. There is, for example, the economical technique, the propaganda technique and political technique, All working together and dependent on one another.

What It Does To Us?

More and more we seek maximum efficiency in all fields of human activity and we tend to think that is because we want to, to make our lives better. We try to rationalize economies and apply theories so that it can potentially yield better results. We vote for politicians who we believe are going to make our life conditions better. We spend enormous amounts of time binge watching the latest shows so we can forget about how boring and meaningless our work and life really is.

This search for maximum efficiency makes us slaves to technique, contrary to the popular notion that we are using it. Technique develops by itself and for it itself. We are forced to accept technical advancement or else we are put at a disadvantage compared to those who have accepted it. There is only an illusion of choice in our technical society. For example, the President doesn’t really have much power as he is limited by technique. May he dare to oppose it and he will suffer the consequences. How, then, does the president explain that the plan that he envisioned and set out to do, ignoring what technique said, failed to come about? No one is going to be happy with it and soon the President will be no more, replaced by someone else more inline with what technique expects of him.

In the economical sphere, almost everybody is convinced of the effectiveness of planning (that is, to an extent). Even some Hayek fanboys tend to be tempted the allow at least some minimal intervention on the markets. We have increasingly more complex and, over time, possibly more accurate models of our economy as technique is perfected. As of now, economical technique, as with other techniques, it is still in its infancy and the results of it’s application still have a lot of uncertainty.

As technique advances, the tendency is to create a more centralized and totalitarian government with a totally planned economy. This is due to the fact that, as technique progresses, it tends to become more and more expensive. As it becomes more expensive, the only institutions with enough wealth to afford expanding technique tend to be monopolistic corporations with a vast amount of wealth and the state. Because of this, Technique prefers to centralize wealth so that it can maximize the funds for its development. A good example of this type of system is China, where the state has ownership of the companies and can choose to invest in a particular area with the help of said companies, for example, Huawei.

Of course, this brings with it unnatural and negative consequences for the individual. For that reason it is necessary to control and satisfy his basic needs so that he doesn’t revolt against the system. In the most severe cases, mass surveillance and state force is used to take control of the situation but, as of now, this method is expensive and not a good solutions long term. Propaganda and education techniques are employed to steer people in the direction that technique wants them to take before any extreme measure is taken. This is to prepare the individual for a great amount of meaningless work and stay still for 8 hours in a desk. Although he is only an object in technique’s view, in a similar idea to Marx’s labour-power, as just a mere commodity to be traded, easily illustrated by the new human resources department of hip and cool companies, he is still human and may not initially be integrated totally to the technological system. May he fail to integrate and become neurotic, the technical system will employ the use of mind altering techniques such as psychology to reintegrate the individual back into the system. Even if what is causing the distress in the person is a byproduct of the technical system, a psychologist won’t point that out, as his is job aiding the individual to be reintegrated back into the technical system. In specific cases, The technical system may resort to the use drugs, doping the individual to be capable of handling the extreme negative effects of the situation he is in and reduce the possibility of breaking down, somewhat similar to lube in a machine. A good example of this is the use of stimulants by the Nazis in the second world war. The soldier were doped with drugs, to endure the fatigue caused by the intensity of blitz krieg.

The technical system makes many, especially young people, to feel aimless, without any purpose in their life. They don’t know what they want to do in the future. This is due to the technique’s increased demand for specialized work, as technique becomes more and more complex. This forces people to specialize. something people didn’t do has much as the times we live in. Vocational guidance was the technical solution developed for this problem. The big difference between the mainstream idea of vocational guidance and the technical counterpart is that the mainstream idea is based on finding the aptitude of the individual and the technical is focused on the adaptability of the individual to what the technical system desires. This has lead to the fields of Humanities and Arts to be considered less valuable than more technical fields. This is reflected when we say jokingly that arts is a waste of time cause and it’s going to leave us homeless. The emphasis of vocational guidance is on insuring the happiness of the individual through adaptation. Vocational guidance is yet another technique to integrate the individual without force into the technical system.

By way of propaganda, technique shapes and molds us without our consent or even awareness that it is taking place. It suppresses our critical faculties, not necessarily because we refuse to acknowledge it but simply because it was put into our heads and everyone around us seems to think in a similar way. this is due to technique acting upon the collective. In turn, suppressing the individuals critical faculties leads to a good social conscience. The individual finds himself agreeing with his friends and workers, thinking that they are all partaking in something worthwhile. This is greatly reflected on Nazi Germany and the communist regime and even the United States during the second world war. In case the propaganda technique fails, it leads to a great increase in neurotic behavior. These reasons give technique a sacred character. We aren’t allowed to question it. We cease to know what is true and what is false and we’d rather go with the flow than being left behind as we are addicted to it as if it was a drug.

Thus, Technique artificially induces a new zone of the forbidden, in contrast to taboos of primitive society which emerge naturally. A good representation of this is the overton window, where, the window’s position on the political spectrum dictates what is deemed acceptable to talk about and discuss. Outside of the window is what is considered radical, taboo or simply wrong, not worth discussing. Although simplistic, the overton window reflects the core concept of ellul’s idea of the sacred.

The final goal of technique is to totally integrate and subjugate the individual. It touches all fields of human activity. His work life, social life, his psychology… everything. This may lead to the total separation of men from real world. This quote from the book best illustrates this: “One of our best-known specialists in diseases of the nervous system writes: “We will be able to modify man’s emotions, desires and thoughts, as we have already done in a rudimentary way with tranquilizers.” It will be possible, says our specialist to produce a conviction or an impression of happiness without any real basis for it. Our man of the golden age, therefore, will be capable of “happiness” amid the worst privations. Why, then, promise us extraordinary comforts, hygiene, knowledge, and nourishment if, by simply manipulating our nervous systems, we can be happy without them? The last meager motive we could possibly ascribe to the technical adventure thus vanishes into thin air through the very existence of technique itself.”

Final Notes

The book offers no hope for what is ahead of us, may we follow down this path. I personally believe that there is a possibility which is something I plan on expanding on. But the mere existence of this book, The technological society, is a hint that there is still hope.
