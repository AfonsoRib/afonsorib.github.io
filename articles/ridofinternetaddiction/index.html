Rid your self of internet addiction

July 8, 2022

No temptation has overtaken you that is not common to man. God is faithful, and he will not let you be tempted beyond your ability, but with the temptation he will also provide the way of escape, that you may be able to endure it. - 1 Corinthians 10:13

Feeling like the internet as been taking a lot of your valuable time in recent years? I will share with you some of the ways I found that helped me escape the addiction and hopefully help you too!

why do we spend so much time on the internet?

With the advent of social media and other platforms whose main objective is making money, the internet quickly turned into a place where you spend most your time on said platforms. The more time you spend on these platforms, the more money they make. Therefore, their aim is to make you spend the majority of your time there and, in a way, hack you brain. If you are a seasoned internet addict, will most likely developed an habit to look at your phone when you get bored even if it’s just for an instant or spend countless hours on YouTube or other websites. That’s their doing (and also yours if you continue to let that happen).

The easy way out!

Thankfully, there is a easy way out! Just turn of the internet. No, I’m serious, please do it. This is the cold turkey method. The best way to break an addiction is by not practicing it. It might feel weird the first few days but trust me, it gets easier as you go on. You will find that you have a lot of things you could do instead of spending time on the internet. If you don’t have that feeling than I’d recommend you try to go outside for a walk or read a book you’d like, something to fill your time up.

A quick warning: Don’t fear missing out on something. Most likely anything won’t happen and if anything does happen it most likely it isn’t important. If you are expecting some important message or email than make sure you receive it and turn off the internet immediately. You may spend more time than expected while you are online looking at cute cats or whatever happens to be on your social media feed.

Reducing our addiction while still using the internet

Nowadays, we are dependent on the internet to our work. That gets in our way to reduce our internet usage. We may find ourselves looking at social media and other websites instead of doing the work that we need to get done. And, when things really need to get done, they end up with a lot of problems and we end up with a lot of stress trying to accomplish something in half of the time we had to do it. That’s not healthy to anyone.

Just like above, the answer most people would give you is simple: “Just don’t do it, lol”. But, sometimes, we rely on social media for something. Let’s say we need to watch a tutorial on YouTube. you watch the tutorial you needed but then look at the side bar and see a lot of videos that caught your attention. You feel tempted and, depending how ingrained your habit of watching YouTube is, you may ending up clicking on it and spending the next thirty minutes watching a YouTube video. either way it’s not going to be the only time you are going to be tempted on the platform.

Breaking the chain

What we want to do is to stop somewhere on the chain of actions that lead you to spend countless hours online without any good reason. Let’s take the example I showed above. What could we do that would help us not spend thirty minutes watching a random YouTube video? As mentioned, above you could just use your will power but, for someone who has an ingrained habit, it may take a great amount of will power. Most of the time you, your primitive brain just takes over and you end up watching the video. Instead, We could just cut the videos on the sidebar of YouTube. That surely would help you not spend some more time than desired. There are a few browser addons that can do this but I would recommend unhook. It has been the best one from my personal experience. Just click the link, choose your browser and then install it. Tweak the settings to your desired preferences and you are ready to go!

Creating scarcity

Another way to create is to artificially create scarcity. What do I mean by this? When something is scarce and valuable to us, we tend to be more careful with how we use it, for example, money. If we limit how much time we have access to internet or certain websites we will tend to be careful with how much time is spent there. For this, I would recommend leechblock. Another addon but this one is meant to limit how much time is spent on certain websites. Experiment with it to find a good amount of time where you can comfortably do the things you want but not spend much more time than desired looking at mostly useless things.

Resources

Now that you got a good look how your internet addiction works and got the basis of how to break it, here are some resources to help you with your journey:

StevenBlack’s hosts

it’s a collection of links that contain harmful content (adware, malware, fake news, gambling, porn and social media) organized in a format meant to be blocked. If you store all these links on your computer’s host file, they will be blocked when you browse the internet. They have a tutorial on where to find the hosts file on your system. click here to check it out. There is also this video tutorial if you are interested.

AfonsoRib’s Hosts

Follows the same scheme as above. It’s just a collection of links that I felt were missing from stevenBlack’s hosts. It blocks a bunch of fediverse websites and other frontends for things like reddit and YouTube. You may want to unblock some fediverse instances if you use it actively.

Unhook - Remove YouTube Recommended Videos

As it was said above, This is a browser addon meant to block youtube recommend sections and much more other things meant to sway your attention while on the platform.

LeechBlock

Another addon that was mentioned above. This one is meant to help you not spend more time than desired in a specific websites of your choice.

Whats next?

So you may be wondering: “I’ve done it. I have reduced my internet usage to a point where I’m a healthier person. What’s next?”. What’s written here can also be applied to other parts of your life. It isn’t strictly applied to the internet. I just made it for the internet as it is what I feel like people and myself mostly struggled with nowadays. So try to apply it to other thing in your life and see if you get good results. Good luck!
