---

For a while I've been wanting to minimize my internet usage. I still spend more time than I should on the internet. I don't really see any reward from it. It's just mindless scrolling through fun facts or (((fake))) news that aren't going change anything for me or, in the worst case, create a pessimistic outlook on life.

For those reasons I decided that I'll just unplug myself from the internet for most of the time I'm at my computer and will only use it on a specific day of the week to download things I deem useful and/or necessary. If I want information on topics, I'll mainly get it from books, documentaries, tutorials or friends and family.

The Internet as been both a blessing and a curse. It allows people to share and access great resources that help you grow as a person but it can be a huge time-sink if you fall for the vast amount of content meant to sway your attention and reprogram you.
